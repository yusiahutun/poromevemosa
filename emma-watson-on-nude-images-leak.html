Emma Watson on nude images leak

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, January 1, 0001

Picture: Getty Images

Emma Watson has spoken out about what she perceives as a "lack of empathy" shown by social media users who reacted to the leaked nude images of celebrities, including her close friend Jennifer Lawrence.

"Even worse than seeing women's privacy violated on social media is reading the accompanying comments that show such a lack of empathy," said Watson via her Twitter account.

Girls creator Lena Dunham expressed similar sentiments, saying: "The 'don't take naked pics if you don't want them online' argument is the 'she was wearing a short skirt' of the web. Ugh."

The comments came after hundreds of celebrities had their privacy violated when a hacker released nude images obtained from an iCloud leak. Those reportedly affected include Kim Kardashian, Rihanna, Mary-Kate Olsen, Cara Delevingne, Kirsten Dunst and Kate Upton.

A spokesperson for Jennifer Lawrence condemned the publication of the images as "a flagrant violation of privacy" and has confirmed that the authorities are looking into tracking down whoever is responsible.

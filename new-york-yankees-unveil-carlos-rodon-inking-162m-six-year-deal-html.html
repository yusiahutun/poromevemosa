New York Yankees unveil Carlos Rodon after inking $162m, six-year deal

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, May 14, 2024

Carlos Rodon debuted his new clean-shaven Yankees look at his official unveiling after signing a $162m, six-year contract with New York.

After a one-year stint with the San Francisco Giants, the starting pitcher made the move to the Bronx for the 2023 season and beyond following reported long-standing interest from the Yankees. 

However, while the 30-year-old gained his pinstripes, he had to lose his facial hair and he looked dramatically different at his press conference Thursday after complying with the Yankees' clean-shaven policy.

The strict appearance policy, introduced by George Steinbrenner in 1976, dictates that players' hair must not touch their collars and that they may have mustaches but no other facial hair.


Rodon, who previously had a short beard and moustache, had completely shaven his facial hair after signing his new deal with the Bronx Bombers. 

Yankees manager Aaron Boone made reference to his new look before Rodon took to the podium to express how much he's relishing the challenge.

15 female Afrobeats artists you should add to your playlist

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, May 25, 2024

Waist-winding, sidestepping, and hip-thrusting music that forces listeners to bop their heads best describes Afrobeats. However, there can't be great music without great artists. Luckily, female Afrobeats artists have proven that West African music is more than a trend. It is a lifestyle, and everyone should keep up.

Afrobeats is one of the most listened-to music genres today. It has broken the territories of Africa and split across the globe. Consequently, female Afrobeats artists have expanded their fan bases across numerous international countries and appeared in some of the biggest music festivals.

15 female Afrobeats artists

Most West African male musicians easily make their way onto the mainstream media compared to their female counterparts. However, there is a huge number of impressive female artists you should add to your playlist. Here are the top 15 female Afrobeats artists according to The Dream Africa:

1. Yemi Alade Yemi

Alade is one of the most famous female Nigerian artists. Even though she is popularly known as Yemi, she was born Yemi Eberechi Alade on 13th March 1989 in Abia State, Nigeria.

Her music incorporates dancehall, highlife, afro, and pop. Some of her best songs include:

  • Taking Over Me
  • Do as I Do
  • Single and Searching
  • Enjoyment
  • Na Gode
  • Ghen Ghen Love

2. Lola Rae

Lola Rae is a super-talented Nigerian singer. She was born on 20th January 1991 in Obalende, Lagos. She rose to fame after releasing her debut single, Watch My Ting Go.

The song attained critical acclaim, earning her a nomination at the 2013 Nigeria Entertainment Awards for the Most Promising Female Act. Her other hit singles include:

  • High
  • Fi Mi Le
  • She Knows He Knows
  • One Time
  • Come and See
  • Watch my Ting Go
  • Africa Rising
  • All Stars

3. Tiwa Savage

Tiwa Savage is one of the most talented female musicians in Nigeria. She was born Tiwatope Omolara Savage on 5th February 1980 in Isale Eko, Lagos State.

She makes her music in Yoruba and English by blending Afrobeats, Afropop, R and B, hip-hop, and pop. Some of her best releases are:

  • Kele Kele Love
  • Love in Yellow
  • Somebody's Son
  • Stamina
  • Pick Up

4. Efya

Jane Fara Fauzzier Afia Boafowaa Yahaya Awindor, popularly known by her stage name, Efya, is an acclaimed Ghanaian singer, songwriter, and actress. She was born on 10 April 1987 in Kumasi, Ghana.

She became famous after participating in the maiden edition of the Stars of the Future talent show. In 2012, she starred in Single and Married. She is known for hit songs such as:

  • One of your Own
  • Best in Me
  • Until The Dawn
  • The One
  • This is Love
  • Make I Love You
  • Wicked Somebody

5. Becca

Rebecca Akosua Acheampomaa Acheampong is one of the best female Afrobeats artists. She is also a songwriter and actress commonly known by her stage name, Becca. She was born on born 15 August 1984.

Becca rose to fame as one of the contestants of TV3's season two Mentor singing competition. Her debut album, Sugar, earned her five Ghana Music Awards nominations. Some of her best tracks are:

  • African Woman
  • Move
  • Yes I Do
  • Forever
  • With You
  • Don't Know
  • Magic

6. MzVee

MzVee is one of the most talented female Afrobeats singers from Ghana. She was born Vera Hamenoo-Kpeda on 23rd June 1992 in Accra.

She specializes in Afro-pop, R&B, and dancehall genres. In 2019, she parted ways with Lynx Entertainment and now works as a solo musician. She has released:

  • Borkor Borkor (Slowly)
  • Dancehall Queen
  • Natural Girl
  • Come and See My Moda

7. Veeiye

Veeiye is a renowned British-Nigerian singer, songwriter, producer, and former BBNaija ex-housemate. Her ability to seamlessly combine British and Nigerian cultures has made her music stand out from other performers.

She began her musical career in 2016. Some of the best songs to date include:

  • Enter my Head
  • Ec*stasy
  • Forbidden Fruit
  • Let Dem Say
  • Do It
  • Know Now
  • Feenin'

8. Tems

Temilade Openiyi is an acclaimed Nigerian singer and songwriter. She was born on 11th June 1995 in Lagos, but her family relocated to the United Kingdom when she was an infant.

At five, her parents separated, thus moving back to Nigeria. Despite having a rough childhood, she has become one of the most successful musicians. Some of her best hits include:

  • Free Mind
  • Replay
  • Try Me
  • Crazy Tings
  • No Woman, No Cry
  • A Kind of Magic
  • Ordinary People

9. Simi

Simi is one of the most famous Afrobeat artists you should add to your playlist. She is a renowned Nigerian singer, songwriter, and actress.

She was born Simisola Bolatito Kosoko on 19th April 1988 in Ojuelegba, Lagos. She began her career as a gospel musician. So far, she has released the following:

10. Ayra Starr

Born Oyinkansola Sarah Aderibigbe, Ayra Starr is a Beninese-born Nigerian singer, former model, and performing artist. She was born on 14th June 2002 in Cotonou, Benin. She was a model before venturing into music. Her hit singles include:

Has Heather Rae El Moussa Left &quot;Selling Sunset&quot; After Season 7?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Since Selling Sunset first began in 2019, Heather Rae El Moussa has been an important part of the show. In recent seasons, the Netflix reality series—focused on high-end Los Angeles real estate brokerage the Oppenheim Group—has followed the real estate agent as she has fallen in love, gotten married, and started a family with husband and HGTV star Tarek El Moussa.

However, Heather’s infrequent appearances in season seven of Selling Sunset have fans wondering whether she’ll be back for future seasons of the show. Here’s what we know so far.

Heather was on maternity leave during season seven.

Heather Rae El Moussa’s brief appearance at the start of Selling Sunset season seven showed her getting ready for the birth of her and Tarek’s first baby together. The couple’s son, Tristan Jay, was born on January 31, 2023, with Tarek sharing on Instagram, “Mama & baby are happy, healthy, tired but doing well. Our hearts are so happy.”

In an emotional Instagram post, Heather revealed she’d experienced a difficult birth, with doctors rushing her to the hospital after the baby stopped moving as much as he should have been. Because of past medical issues, Heather’s water didn't break on its own, so labor was induced. “It all happened really fast,” she wrote on Instagram. “Tristan’s heart rate was dropping very low every time I’d push & was staying low. Which was terrifying.” Luckily, her baby boy was born safe and well, but it’s clear that Heather endured a traumatic time leading up to his birth.

During an interview with E! News (via People) in March 2023, Heather revealed she was upset that Selling Sunset was filming without her. “Season seven is filming right now, but I’ve been off maternity leave and I’ve been excited to get back to work, and so far I have not been called back,” she said. She added, “It’s been a little frustrating. So [I’m] not sure what’s been going on."

Heather and Tarek launched their own reality show.

Another reason Heather might not be as involved in future seasons of Selling Sunset is that she’s since launched her own reality show on HGTV, The Flipping El Moussas, in collaboration with Tarek. Tarek first rose to fame due to his work on HGTV’s Flip or Flop, which he starred on alongside ex-wife Christina Hall.

It makes sense that Heather and Tarek would want to combine their expertise into their own reality show, but there may be conflicts with Netflix’s Selling Sunset production schedule in the future. In July 2023, HGTV renewed The Flipping El Moussas for a second season, meaning Heather will be spending a lot of her time filming that series.

Way back in August 2022, Oppenheim Group co-founder Brett Oppenheim discussed Heather’s future on Selling Sunset, following the announcement of her new HGTV show. “I think it’s TBD,” he told E! News. “I think for now she’s able to do both.” Twin brother and co-founder Jason Oppenheim also weighed in, saying, “I know she’s still going to be at the Oppenheim Group. I think that’s with her and production and Netflix and all that.” He continued, “I love Heather. I’m super supportive of Heather and Tarek and their new show … I hope she continues to film with us.”

Heather reacted to Selling Sunset’s season-seven poster snub.

In October 2023, Heather took to Instagram to react to the latest promo poster for Selling Sunset, from which she was mysteriously missing. Joking about her absence from the photo, Heather wrote, “Looks like I got pushed in the water… its a good thing I can swim! Congrats to my girls on S7! 💗 You may see me in a few episodes….. 💁🏼‍♀️”

Luckily, Heather seemed to take the snub in good spirits, and showed nothing but support for her castmates. Still, it can’t have been easy to see the glossy picture featuring the rest of the show’s stars, but not her.

Heather was diagnosed with an autoimmune disorder.

Also in October 2023, Heather revealed she’d been diagnosed with Hashimoto’s disease, an autoimmune disorder that causes a person’s immune system to attack their own thyroid gland. This can result in symptoms such as weight gain, fatigue, joint pain, and a slowed heart rate, the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases says.

Speaking to Today.com, Heather explained, “I remember saying to my assistant, ‘I feel like I’m dead.’ … My brain was so tired. My body was so tired. I was exhausted all the time and no amount of sleep could make it better.” At first, Heather assumed the symptoms were postpartum issues, as she had recently given birth to Tristan Jay. “I was like, ‘I’m probably just foggy because of mom brain,’” she said.

When she started struggling to produce milk, Heather was referred to a doctor who diagnosed her with Hashimoto’s disease. Fortunately, Heather told Today.com, she has been “feeling much better” since then. Still, it’s clear she has had a lot to deal with throughout 2023.

Will Heather be back for season eight?

If Selling Sunset returns to Netflix for an eighth season, there’s hope that Heather will be back on our screens. Not only has she made it clear she’d love to return to the series, her costars have also showed their support for the new mom.

In an interview with E! News (via Page Six) in July 2023, Selling Sunset’s Bre Tiesi said, “I absolutely adore Heather.” She continued, “I’m very sad to hear that she is not going to be as big a part [of the show]. … We got really close.” Tiesi also said she hadn’t been told whether Heather would be returning: “They kinda keep us separate from all of that.” Still, we have our fingers crossed she’ll be back!

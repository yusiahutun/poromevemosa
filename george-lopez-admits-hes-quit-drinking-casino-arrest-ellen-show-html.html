'Since then I have not had a drop of alcohol': George Lopez admits he's quit drinking after casino arrest on Ellen show

George Lopez might have given up the bottle, but his endless stream of jokes continue to pour forth.

The 52-year-old comedian confessed in his funny way that his recent arrest for public intoxication in a Canadian casino was a wake-up call during Thursday's segment of The Ellen DeGeneres Show.

Lopez said he hasn't had a hair of the dog that bit him since the February 27 incident and follows a more sober path these days.

Sober: George Lopez revealed on Thursday's The Ellen DeGeneres Show that he hasn't had a drop of alcohol since his embarrassing arrest last month at a Canadian casino

'Since then I have not had a drop of alcohol,' Lopez told Ellen. 'That was a nice going-away party for that guy, and then I'm the new George now sitting here in front of you.'

The amiable chat show host began by asking what he has been doing lately.

'Well, Ellen, recently I lifted myself off the casino floor in Canada,' Lopez told her good-naturedly. 'You know how they always say whatever happens in Vegas stays in Vegas? Whatever happens in Canada, everybody knows."'

High time for a snooze: The comedian passed out on the casino floor and police subsequently arrested him for public intoxication on February 27

Polite wake-up call: Lopez said that authorities gently roused him from his slumber to say he was under arrest

Lost his way: The funny man joked that he was on his way to his hotel room but 'just missed it by 35 floors'

 

'I was a bit intoxicated in Canada,' Lopez went on. 'The authorities are much politer in Canada. You know, "Sir, wake up. Sir, you're under arrest." Very polite.

'And they told me, they said, "Sir, this behavior cannot continue. And if it does continue, we would be honored if you would be mayor of Toronto,"' the comedian joked.

He was referring to Toronto's current mayor, Rob Ford, who recently admitted he used crack cocaine 'in one of [his] drunken stupors.'

In stitches: Ellen found George's jokes amusing and so did the audience

Bieber bashing: Lopez threw another punch Justin Bieber's way when he compared his incident to the pop star's recent troubles

'Hilarious,' Ellen said. 'That's nice that they were polite and everything worked out okay. So you were asleep on the floor of the casino?'

'You know, I was on my way to my room - I just missed it by 35 floors,' Lopez quipped. 'The casino carpet is so loud. I'm like, how the hell can I sleep on that carpet? 

'It was so loud, you know, and guys were betting on whether I would get up. And then one person came by and said, "I didn't know the Dog Whisperer [Cesar Millan] drank that much."'

Lopez added: 'You know, just because Mexicans invented tequila doesn't mean we can handle it. Apparently I'm a bigger Justin Beiber fan than I thought I was.'

In all earnestness: The 52-year-old said the man you see today is the 'new George' and he's here to stay

That statement poked fun at the 20-year-old pop star's recent troubles with the law involving a suspected DUI and drug use.

Lopez's quip echoed a similar joke made during his stand-up routine earlier this month when he said, 'I did to Canada what Justin Bieber does to America.'

George had performed at the casino earlier in the evening before the incident occurred.

The former late night show host split from his wife Ann in 2010 after 17 years of marriage and one daughter, Mayan, finalising their divorce a year later.

Ann had donated one of her kidneys to Lopez in 2005 after a genetic condition caused his kidneys to deteriorate.

King of comedy: The entertainer is currently taking his comic routine across the country; he was pictured doing a show in Hollywood, Florida on January 4

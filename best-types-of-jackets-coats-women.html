10 Types of Jackets and Coats for Women

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, February 10, 2024

Outerwear is an essential part of every woman's wardrobe, for obvious reasons. The best coats and jackets are closet necessities, acting as layers for warmth or offering protection from the elements. You might turn to a trench coat on a rainy day or a puffer jacket when subzero temperatures hit. However, great outerwear has the ability to be far more than just practical.

“Outerwear is functional, but at its best, it's also fashionable,” George Fountas, Vice President and General Merchandise Manager of RTW, Outerwear, Swim, IA and Little BG at Bergdorf Goodman, tells BAZAAR. “Being the last layer you put on, it's also the first thing that people see and in cold climates, it might be the only layer they see.” Think about your relationship to outerwear as so. Firstly, jackets and coats are an acting necessity, but also as toppers that bring a whole look together.

“I've heard this quote attributed to a few different people: ‘In New York, your coat is your car’,” Fountas says. “From that perspective, I'm a believer in investing in great outerwear, and perhaps, unlike a car, having multiple options!” Looking for durable styles and superior quality is important, since often our jackets and coats are worn weekly, sometimes even daily, depending on the time of year.

Different coats serve different purposes, so knowing which are the must-haves will be beneficial in the long run, to find order and balance in a well-curated closet. “Most styles of outerwear are like the wheel, not needing to be reinvented,” Fountas explains. “When a shape works, it just works and from there, you just maybe need to tweak the fabric or color might make it less classic.”

Below, the 10 best jacket and coat styles for women.

Denim Jackets

Nothing is more timeless than your favorite pair of jeans, and the same goes for a denim jacket. You will turn to this forever staple when the weather is still mild, best worn during spring, fall and those chillier nights of summer. Layer it over your favorite tee, lightweight sweater or chiffon dress for a casual option that never goes out of style. Whether light wash or dark is your thing, if you haven't invested in one already, there is no time like the present.

Leather Biker Jackets

“A perfecto leather never goes out of style and this is a great in-between-seasons jacket to layer over anything,” Fountas tells BAZAAR. The ultimate in versatility, a leather moto jacket will take you across various seasons and temperatures, all with the signature cool factor of a heritage biker look. Invest in one truly well-made jacket and you will it have the rest of your life. From cropped, convertible and oversized, there is a fit that will be right for you.

Bomber Jackets

Bomber jackets (or flight jackets) have come a long way since their introduction during WWI, as a military garb that kept pilots warm in open airplane cockpits. Today, they are part of the everyday street style vernacular, notable for their collarless, zip-front design, generally made from nylon or other sporty-feeling fabrics. Some are simple in construction, some are more utilitarian with extra pockets and detailing, but either way, bomber jackets are a great option for cooler days of spring and fall, when you need a light layer but nothing as heavyweight as a puffer.

Puffer Jackets

“Within the puffer jacket category, choices are endless,” Fountas says. “There are various options of length, puffiness, color, print. It’s the best way to stay warm on the slopes or brutal sub-freezing day.” Known for their quilted and oversized shapes that hit around the hipline, puffers are filled with warm, insulating materials that keep the body heat central. Whether worn with jeans on the weekend or as a more technical style for a snowy, mountain adventure, the puffer is a necessity for the winter months.

Raincoats

“A raincoat with a hood” is at the top of Fountas' list. “Stop worrying about losing an umbrella and just add this layer. A great style will actually make you enjoy the rain,” he explains. Anorak shapes made from lightweight and water-repellent fabrics are key, and a hooded shape is vital to protect from the elements. Be sure to keep your color choice versatile, like black, navy or khaki, to ensure optimal styling opportunities, whenever those last minute rain showers hit.

Trench Coats

Best known for their khaki colorway, double-breasted design and waist-cinching belt, trench coats were another military staple that have become increasingly popular over the years. Most brands today have a trench in their offerings, for good reason. “Trench coats might double as your raincoat depending on fabric and treatment but it's also one of the best classic options when you need an extra layer and want to look effortless,” Fountas tells us. Whether it be a modernized original shape or a variation on the theme, such as longer lengths, collarless or even embroidered, this is another must-have buy.

Cashmere Coats

“A cashmere coat in a classic color like black, navy or camel is an upfront investment that will be worth it, after you calculate the price per wear,” Fountas says. “I suggest a longer silhouette.” Generally, cashmere coats are made in simple, signature shapes, such as a double-breasted peacoat style or a wrap with tie sash. They are extremely warm and soft, thanks to the luxe material, and can be effortlessly layered with a chunky sweater during the winter months. These coats are another timeless silhouette, that look smart and professional, and can take you from work to dinner and beyond.

Leopard Coats

From Barbra Streisand to Kate Moss, the leopard coat has become a favorite for minimalist and maximalist dressers alike, making it a closet essential. Classic menswear silhouettes or trench styles are typical for this "neutral" print, and these days, faux fur has become the fabrication du jour. Whether worn with a turtleneck and trousers or mismatched with a dark floral dress, you will find this coat is the ideal centerpiece for all your fall and winter outfits, whether casual or dressed up.

“Another must-have is a statement coat,” Fountas adds. “Exactly what it sounds like, live a little.” A statement coat can mean anything from heavily embroidered and bedazzled to a floral print and beyond. Bright, bold colors will take center stage. Whether it is an evening coat that sparkles and shines, or a day coat that works well with the best basics (i.e. jeans and a plain sweater), a statement coat is ideal for special occasions or to cure the mid-winter blahs.

Parkas

Similar to puffer jackets, parkas are generally insulated, for the ultimate in cold-weather dressing. However, parkas take on a longer shape, rather than a puffer jackets' cropped silhouette, ensuring that more of the body is covered on those extra cold days. Either zip-front or button-front, parkas are essential when the temperatures hit subzero and a snow storm is on the horizon. When choosing your own, look for the style's particular fill count, to differentiate between the warmness levels.

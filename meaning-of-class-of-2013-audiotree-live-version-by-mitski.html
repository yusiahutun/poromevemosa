Meaning of Class Of 2013 (Audiotree Live Version) by Mitski

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, July 11, 2024

The Mitski song "Class of 2013 (Audiotree Live Version)" is a haunting and introspective piece that delves into the complex feelings of uncertainty, vulnerability, and the desire for stability. At its core, the song speaks to the universal struggle of navigating adulthood and the longing for a sense of belonging and security.One standout lyric that captures the essence of the song is "Mom, I'm tired, can I sleep in your house tonight?". This line evokes a sense of weariness and the longing for comfort, safety, and familiarity. It speaks to the inherent desire to return to a place where one feels protected and loved, suggesting a longing for the simplicity and security of childhood.Another powerful lyric is "And I'll leave once I figure out how to pay for my own life too". This lyric taps into the fear of independence and the weight of shouldering the responsibility of building one's own life. It encapsulates the pressure and overwhelm that comes with trying to navigate the practicalities of adulthood, such as finances and career choices. Through this lyric, the song explores the theme of self-reliance and the anxious pursuit of stability.One surprising interpretation of the line "Mom, would you wash my back? This once, and then we can forget" is the notion of surrendering control and vulnerability. This lyric suggests a longing for temporary respite from the weight of adult responsibilities, allowing oneself to lean on another's support. It represents a momentary retreat from independence and a desire to be cared for, mirroring the longing for the comfort of a mother's nurturing presence.Another lyric that carries weight is "Mom, am I still young? Can I dream for a few months more?". This line evokes a sense of longing for the freedom and innocence of youth. It explores the fear of growing up too fast and losing the ability to dream boldly. It speaks to the pressure to conform and settle into the routines and expectations of adulthood, hinting at the desire to preserve the naivety and possibility that comes with youth.The overarching theme of the song revolves around the struggle to find one's place in the world, grappling with the desires for stability, support, and the preservation of youthful dreams. It delves into the complexities of adulthood and the yearning for the safety and comfort of childhood.Ultimately, "Class of 2013 (Audiotree Live Version)" offers a poignant exploration of the human experience and the vulnerabilities that come with growing up. Through its thought-provoking lyrics and emotive delivery, the song invites listeners to reflect on their own journey from youth to adulthood, reminding us of the universal longing for a sense of security and belonging in an ever-changing world.

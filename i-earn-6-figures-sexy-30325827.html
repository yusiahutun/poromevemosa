Despite her success so far, the Vietnamese-Canadian beauty's alternative career did not go down well with her 'traditional' family.

Speaking exclusively to Daily Star, Asia Doll explained how her unconventional job comes with sacrifices.

She said: "I started creating content five years ago, but only started getting traction in the last year.

"I started for fun, and never thought I could make a full time living out of this.

"I didn’t seriously start putting in the work until Covid hit and I couldn’t work at my day job.

"I was a full-time stripper before that, so that wasn’t great for social distancing.

"Covid made me realise that I prefer working from home, and even after Covid you have the freedom of working from anywhere in the world if you want to travel.

"When I started, I had months as low as four figure months, and as I’ve put in more work, I've had months as high as six figure months.

"It takes a lot more work than I originally anticipated to succeed."

Taking the risk of ditching sandwiches for stripping, then swapping it for shooting sexy scenes, Asia had to deal with coming to terms of family disapproval.

She had to change her content creator name as her relatives did not want to be 'associated' with her.

Sadly, the stigma around sex work made her family relationship difficult.

Although, that will not stop Asia Doll from doing what she loves.

"Asia Doll was a nickname I had a while ago and I had to pick a different name than my real name because my Vietnamese family is quite traditional and don't want to be associated with me publicly," she explained.

"I totally understand the stigma around sex work as I come from a rather traditional and strict family."

She continued: "And they always tell me to not tell anyone from my ethnicity what I do for a living because people will talk and associate me to them.

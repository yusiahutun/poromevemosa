Psychology of Money: How to Save Money in 2023?

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Monday, February 19, 2024

Most people experience stress at least once a month due to issues related to money or savings. This is not only due to external factors, such as low income, job loss, price increases, etc., but also because of the psychology of money in the head. Why Is It Hard to Save Money?

Saving money is not only about financial strategies, it also involves understanding the psychology of money and consciously changing behavior. Reasons why people find it difficult to save money:

  • Low earnings. It is very difficult to create an airbag or save money if earnings barely cover household expenses.
  • Lack of financial literacy. Many people have limited knowledge about personal finance, budgeting, and savings strategies.
  • Emergency costs. Unexpected events, such as a medical emergency, car repairs, or the loss of a job, can quickly drain savings.
  • Unplanned expenses. Impulse buying, emotional spending, and lack of self-control can lead to unplanned purchases.
  • Lack of financial discipline. Constant saving requires discipline and self-control. Without these qualities, it is easy to fall into the habit of overspending.

10 Tips to understand the psychology of money to save:

Here are ten tips to form financial strategies and create healthy and effective money-saving habits:

  • Set clear goals: Having specific financial goals like a vacation or buying property or personal goals like financial freedom or material stability gives you a reason and motivation to save money.
  • Automate your savings: Set up automatic transfers from your bank account to a savings account. Most banking apps allow you to set up automatic deposits and transfers every month.
  • Track your spending habits: Keep track of where your money is going and identify areas where you can cut those costs.
  • Use cash envelopes: Divide certain amounts of money into different expense categories and use envelopes to physically separate the money. This visual method will help keep costs under control.
  • Implement the 24 hour rule: For purchases that are not urgent, please allow 24 hours before purchasing. This gives you time to evaluate whether a purchase is necessary.
  • Practice frugality: Look for ways to cut costs without sacrificing quality. This may include sales, using coupons, or looking for alternatives to expensive habits.
  • Make only conscious expenses: Before making a purchase, consider whether it aligns with your values and long-term goals. Conscious spending reduces impulse buying.
  • Keep motivated with rewards: The psychology of money requires you to stop, feel the progress — and praise yourself for it. In fact, celebrating even a small success will remind you of your goals.
  • Spend less than you earn: If every penny that goes into your account goes to purchases, you will have nothing to save. Each month, keep track of how much you have to spend and make sure you don’t spend more than you earn.
  • Don’t let ads influence you uncontrollably: Do not read magazines or blogs that promote luxury or fashion items. Limit time on sites where there is a lot of advertising: from TV to social networks.

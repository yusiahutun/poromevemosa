Who Is Jasmine Roth's Spouse, Brett Roth?

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, August 7, 2024

In the inaugural season of her HGTV show, Hidden Potential, Jasmine Roth, 33, has uncovered just that: spaces with potential that were previously unused or unseen. But one part of her life that remains rarely seen (at least onscreen), is her husband, Brett Roth (also 33).

While similar programs on the network star spouses, Jasmine's partner in life is noticeably not her partner at work.

"Early on, I think [the producers] were trying to fit us into that kind of prior mold of other shows where they do follow that storyline, and it just didn't really work," Jasmine told CountryLiving.com. "He's running a business—a big business—and I'm doing my own thing."

That "thing" is constructing houses for her Huntington Beach, California, company, Built Custom Homes. Brett owns a national staffing company with his family.

"So he gets up every day and puts his suit on, and I get up every day and put my work boots on." Jasmine explained. "I think it's cool to each be able to have our own careers and be really supportive of each other."

That comes naturally for the couple, who met at Northeastern University in Boston, where they both majored in entrepreneurship.

"We kind of did it backwards, right?" Jasmine asked Brett, who was sitting nearby at the time of our phone conversation. Before they even started dating, they moved in together—as roommates. Brett even had a girlfriend at the time, and Jasmine had a boyfriend. But when Jasmine's grandmother visited and Brett cooked an incredible dinner, she must have spotted some sort of spark.

"My grandma told me later that she leaned over to my mom and said, 'He's the one.' So my grandma's claim to fame is that she called it before we even knew."

By Christmas, the roomies knew, too. In 2013, they tied the knot in a destination wedding in Park City, Utah, the same city Jasmine called from the day of our interview (they now have a tiny mountain house in the area).

"We didn't move in to live together forever, but it just kind of happened," Jasmine said.

So did Jasmine's eventual career. The same year they married, the Roths finished building their first house. Originally, they were looking to buy, but lofty real estate prices led them to decide to build instead. The young couple was ambitious, constructing not one, but two houses—one to live in, and the other to sell—in what little free time they had outside of their full-time corporate jobs.

But after two years and not much progress, it just made sense for Jasmine to step away from her job as a consultant in nonprofit management to focus on the project—and she hasn't gone back since.

Getting "discovered" on Instagram and filming her own HGTV show was surreal for both Jasmine and Brett. Both are "huge HGTV fans" and have seen nearly every episode of every show.

Now, in the wake of season one, the couple is busy building their new house, four blocks away from their first home. The bigger lot will allow for a pool and a bigger garage, as well as plenty of space for Brett to grill and smoke meats (he's a self-described "Meatatarian"), for Jasmine to entertain (she's hosted Hidden Potential viewing parties every week for friends and family), and for the pups to play.

Although Jasmine and Brett, who will celebrate their five-year wedding anniversary this September, don't have kids ("yet," she said), they are parents to two dogs: Nala the bulldog and Tiger the rescue chihuahua.

Early life

Daphne Nur Oz was born February 17, 1986, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA. She is the eldest child in her family, born to television personalities Mehmet Oz and Lisa Oz (née Lemole). Daphne’s paternal grandparents, Suna (née Atabay) and Mustafa Öz emigrated from Konya Province, Turkey.

The television personality has three siblings, Arabella Oz, Zoe Oz, and Oliver Oz. Daphne and her siblings were raised in Cliffside Park, New Jersey. She attended Dwight Englewood School, where she graduated in 2004. Later she attended Princeton College, where she graduated in 2008 with a degree in Near Eastern Studies.

Daphne Nur Oz is also a graduate of the Institute for Integrative Nutrition. In addition, she has a culinary degree from The Natural Gourmet Institute.

Television career

Daphne was one of the five co-hosts of the daytime talk show The Chew on the ABC channel during the show's first six seasons (2011 to 2017). The famous show won her Daytime Emmy Award, and she shared the 2015 award for the Outstanding Informative Talk Show Host with her Chew co-hosts.

Author

Oz's first book was The Dorm Room Diet: The 10-Step Program for Creating A Healthy Lifestyle Plan That Really Works (Newmarket Press, 2006), a National Bestseller. She used tips and tricks to successfully navigate the unique danger zones of college life to create a healthy lifestyle and sustainably lose 40lbs.

The book advanced her approach to avoid the Freshman 15, as she gives advice on developing healthy habits while in college that may benefit the student through life.

The book was publicized by multiple media outlets, including The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, People, The Washington Post, Reader's Digest, Teen Vogue, Cosmo Girl! and Seventeen.

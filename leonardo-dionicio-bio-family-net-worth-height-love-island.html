Leonardo Dionicio On Love Island USA Season 5

Love Island USA season five premiered on July 18, 2023, and it saw a cast of 12 singles including Leonardo Dionicio searching for love whilst also aiming for that $100,000 cash prize.

Leonardo’s game plan this season was to sway away the gals with his eye contact, jokes, and his fluent Spanish. There will be no cheesy pickup lines from his side as he believes it is what turns women off.

For those new to the show, Love Island is set in Fiji where a group of contestants, known as “Islanders,” live in a villa under constant video surveillance. To stay in the villa, Islanders must be in a romantic relationship with another contestant. They couple up based on first impressions but later have to “re-couple” during special ceremonies, deciding whether to stay with their current partner or switch. While in the villa, Islanders have phones to communicate with each other via text and receive updates on challenges, dumpings, and re-couplings.

The show features various games and challenges to test physical and mental abilities, with winners receiving special prizes like a night at the Hideaway or a date. Islanders can be eliminated for remaining single after a re-coupling or through public votes on the Love Island app. In the final week, the public votes to decide the winning couple, who receive two envelopes – one with $100,000 and the other empty. The partner with the money envelope can choose to share it or keep it, testing trust and commitment.

Joining Leonardo for the fifth installment of Love Island USA were his co-stars Anna Kurdys, Carmen Kocourek, Carsten “Bergie” Bergersen, Destiny Davis, Hannah Wright, Jasmine Sklavanitis, Kassy Castillo, Keenan Anunay, Marco Donatelli, Vickala “Kay Kay” Gray, and Victor Gonzalez.

Leonardo Dionicio Family

Leonardo Dionicio Job

Leonardo is a Salesman who loves his job. Find more about his profession on his Linkedin @leonardo-dionicio.

Prior to this, Leonardo was a three-year varsity baseball player (2017-2019) at Hall High School under head coach Nick Marsh. He then attended St. Thomas More High School for his senior year where he received All-Conference honors and was named the captain of his baseball team. There, he hit .317 in his senior year with 10 steals, both of which led the team to victory.

Besides baseball, Leonardo is also interested in singing. In fact, the reality star has already released his first single, “Make You Mine”, to show off a bit of his talent. You can stream his song on Spotify.

5 Minutes With: Lily Aldridge

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, August 16, 2024

Lily Aldridge is back for her second collection with Velvet and the supermodel, mom and rockstar wife is mining inspiration from her adopted hometown of Nashville for a line of tees, dresses, jackets and all-in-ones. BAZAAR chatted with Aldridge about style, why becoming a mom heightened her fashion sense and the pieces she wears over and over again.

Harper's Bazaar: How would you describe your design aesthetic?

Lily Aldridge: My collection is definitely very laid-back, easy to wear, everyday cool clothes.

HB: What did you find after the first season of moving on? Did your approach to design change at all?

LA: The first collection was very successful so I just continued making clothes I wanted to wear, and following my gut, not trying to be too trendy or anything. Just making cool, timeless pieces that really anyone could wear.

HB: Have you found that becoming a mom has affected your style?

LA: Actually I feel I've become way more into fashion, my style and everything since becoming a mother. I don't know why, I just love clothes even more now, if that's possible.

HB: I think it's really sweet that you name each piece after someone that you love. What brought that idea about?

LA: With the first collection, I was like oh my god, I love this piece, it reminds me of my daughter, why don't we name it Dixie? And then it just kind of evolved from there. And this next collection I just wanted to do the same thing and I really tried to match the pieces with people I thought would love the piece. It's really fun, it just makes it a little more personal.

HB: How would you describe your personal style?

LA: That's like, the hardest question to answer because personal style changes all the time but generally I try to be as effortless as possible when I'm getting dressed. I just like being cool and easy, whether it's classic or more on trend, it changes every day.

HB: And what are some of the heroes of your wardrobe?

LA: I'm obsessed with my Chloé susanna boots, I just think they're the coolest boot ever. They just make everything cool. I have black with gold studs and just love them. My Rag & Bone wide brim fedora is something I always have in my suitcase when I'm traveling and my Givenchy Antigona purse. Kind of what I've been wearing a lot lately.

HB: Are you trend-oriented at all? Do you pay attention to what's happening on the runway?

LA: Yes of course, I see all the shows, I'm very involved, I'm with the clothes. it's part of the job and you can't help but be influenced by the trends. You know, there are trends in my collection too, like the crop top. It's what I want to wear this season because it's what is on trend but you know, theres, a difference between following trends and being super trendy, I think.

HB: Sometimes trends are just good ideas.

LA: Yeah exactly. I can't wait for it to be spring and wear a crop top and slouchy pants and those kind of chunky Birkenstock-y kind of sandals. Very on trend.

HB: You're from California but you live in the South now, how has the aesthetic down there influenced you?

LA: Nashville actually has such great style, the people that live down here. I get to be in NY, Nashville, LA—it all changes how I dress. My influences have changed depending on where I am. But Nashville is very coo,l everyone dresses very easy and effortlessly. It's surprisingly very hipster down here.

HB: Tell me a little bit about your thoughts on your modeling career right now. Are you just taking projects you're really passionate about? What has your approach been?

LA: Yeah I have two huge projects coming out and I can't talk about either of them.

HB: So do you just wait to do things you're really excited about?

LA: Well, yeah, there's always amazing things happening. I'm shooting a Victoria's Secret commercial with Michael Bay next week and then I have two big projects coming out. One comes out in two weeks and there's another one I'm about to do so you know there's always exciting things and I'm very blessed.

HB: How do you find that balance between being a mom and a wife and having a career?

LA: Being a mom is definitely my number one priority. That's it, period. Everything else I'm just blessed to do and I love my job and I can pick and choose the days I can work which is, you know, incredibly lucky.

HB: And do you have any fun spring break plans coming up?

LA: We are going to Mallorca for a little bit of the summer so I'm very excited for that.

HB: And are you walking in any shows during fashion month?

LA: I just got a call about maybe doing an exclusive so we'll see.

The Lily Aldridge for Velvet collection retails for $60 to $190 and is available now at velvet-tees.com.

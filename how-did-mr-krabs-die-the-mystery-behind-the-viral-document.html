How Did Mr. Krabs Die? The Mystery Behind the Viral Document

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, July 17, 2024

Mr. Krabs, the avaricious proprietor of the Krusty Krab restaurant and a prominent character in the beloved animated series SpongeBob SquarePants, has found himself at the center of a viral document alleging his murder by either his employee SpongeBob or his rival Plankton. Titled “The Trial of SpongeBob SquarePants,” this document serves as an educational classroom activity that tasks students with evaluating the evidence and testimonies surrounding Mr. Krabs’ supposed demise and forming arguments either for or against SpongeBob’s culpability.

However, many fans of the show have been left perplexed and disconcerted by this document, questioning its veracity and seeking clarification on Mr. Krabs’ fate within the series. In this article, we will delve into the origins and contents of this document while shedding light on the actual events concerning Mr. Krabs in SpongeBob SquarePants.

The Origin and Contents of the Document

“The Trial of SpongeBob SquarePants” appears to be designed as an educational exercise suitable for classroom use. The primary document that has garnered attention is a concise two-page PDF outlining Mr. Krabs’ death, accompanied by a compilation of evidence and witness statements pertaining to the case. When viewed in isolation, this document exudes an air of mystery and even disquiet.

However, an extended 11-page PDF variant of the same document exists, replete with worksheets and instructions explicitly detailing the division of students into teams, each tasked with representing either the defense or prosecution. The objective of this exercise appears to be encouraging students to scrutinize the presented evidence objectively, setting aside their preconceived knowledge of the show, and ultimately determining the true perpetrator behind Mr. Krabs’ alleged murder.

According to the document’s narrative, Mr. Krabs was discovered lifeless inside his restaurant by Squidward, who responded to a scream and hurried to the scene. The coroner’s report attributed Mr. Krabs’ demise to blood loss from a throat laceration inflicted by a metal spatula. A blood-stained spatula was found near Mr. Krabs’ body, suggesting its involvement in the crime.

Additionally, Mr. Krabs exhibited indications of blunt-force trauma to the back of his head, indicating he had been struck with a hard object before the stabbing occurred. The restaurant’s floor was slick with cooking grease, and investigators identified several footprints, including those belonging to SpongeBob. Notably, SpongeBob’s footprints did not lead into the pool of blood near Mr. Krabs’ lifeless form.

While there was no evidence of a break-in at the establishment, both the cash register and the safe housing the confidential Krabby Patty recipe had been emptied. Furthermore, Mr. Krabs had a minor cut on one of his claws, which medical experts determined had occurred several weeks prior to his demise.

The document also features statements from various witnesses, including Patrick, Sandy, Mrs. Puff, Pearl, Larry, and Plankton. Some individuals possessed alibis establishing their whereabouts at the time of the incident, while others harbored motives or suspicions regarding SpongeBob or Plankton. Ultimately, the document challenges students to discern which pieces of evidence and testimonies hold relevance and credibility.

The Actual Fate of Mr. Krabs in the Show

It is essential to clarify that the document is not an official part of the SpongeBob SquarePants franchise and does not reflect the actual storyline of the show. Within the series, Mr. Krabs remains alive and thriving; he has not met his demise at the hands of anyone, including SpongeBob or Plankton

. In fact, Mr. Krabs has continued to make appearances in several episodes following the creation of the document, such as “Krusty Koncessionaires,” “Dream Hoppers,” “Knock Knock Who’s There?,” and “The Patrick Star Show.”

Nonetheless, it is worth mentioning that the show has featured instances where Mr. Krabs faced perilous situations or experienced temporary “deaths” within alternate realities or imaginative scenarios. For instance, in “One Coarse Meal,” he grapples with severe stress and paranoia when threatened by Plankton. In “SpongeBob’s Last Stand,” he, along with SpongeBob and Patrick, is inadvertently run over by a bulldozer while trying to protect Jellyfish Fields from destruction.

In “The SpongeBob Movie: Sponge Out of Water,” he and most of his friends are disintegrated by Burger-Beard’s cannon. In “SpongeBob in RandomLand,” he and Squidward are consumed by a giant realistic fish head. In “SpongeBob’s Big Birthday Blowout,” he is flattened by a giant cake along with all the attendees at SpongeBob’s surprise party.

Importantly, these events are typically resolved through plot devices or revealed to be non-canonical or purely products of imagination, leaving the continuity of the show unaltered. Mr. Krabs retains his role as an integral character in the SpongeBob SquarePants universe, and there is no indication that he will be permanently removed from the series anytime soon.

Conclusion

“The Trial of SpongeBob SquarePants” is a viral document alleging the murder of Mr. Krabs by SpongeBob or Plankton and challenges students to construct arguments either for or against SpongeBob’s guilt based on the provided evidence and testimonies.

However, it is vital to emphasize that this document does not depict actual events in the SpongeBob SquarePants series. Mr. Krabs is very much alive in the show and has only encountered near-death situations or temporary “deaths” in certain alternate realities or imaginative scenarios.

As a central character in the series, fans can rest assured that Mr. Krabs will continue to be a fixture in the SpongeBob SquarePants universe for the foreseeable future.

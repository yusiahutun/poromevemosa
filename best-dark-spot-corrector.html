The 12 Best Dark Spot Correctors of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, August 11, 2024

Dark spots are a famously infuriating foe, and this equal opportunist strikes across skin tones, types, and ages. Like many antagonizers, once excess melanin forms on the skin's surface and dark spots appear, they're in no rush to leave. The overproduction of melanin is your body’s natural defense against aggravating environmental situations, like UV radiation, or lifestyle shifts, like stress or acne, but it takes something stronger than sunscreen to reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation.

Brimming with patience and determination, we decided to fight the good fight against hyperpigmentation by testing the most popular dark spot correctors, from vitamin C serums to at-home peel pads, on our own spotted skin for the next 12 weeks. Our team of InStyle editors spanned a wide range of ages, skin types, concerns, and textures, evaluated each one, starting with how long it took to visibly zap our marks and the extent to which our spots disappeared at the end of the 12-week trial. We asked ourselves if the product evened out our complexions’ tone and texture and whether our skin looked more plump and firm. Then we scored and ranked each one to curate this list of the very best, most effective options to brighten your complexion.

Best Overall

PCA SKIN Hydroquinone-Free Face Serum

PCA SKIN

What We Love: The super effective formula ditches super harsh ingredients in favor of soothing ones, such as fruit extracts, to mollify our angry, irritated skin and effectively banish dark spots, acne, and uneven texture quicker than we expected.

What We Don’t Love: The formula may leave behind a subtle tingling sensation if it’s applied over broken areas of skin. 

We had high, arguably unrealistic, expectations entering this test, and a long list of what we wanted from a dark spot corrector, including everything from fading stubborn acne scars to balancing our complexion’s texture and tone. Oh, and we wanted these wishes granted fast — to our delight, the PCA Skin Hydroquinone-Free Pigment Gel Face Serum fulfilled our dreams on all of these fronts. 

We knew it was a winner from the start when the witch hazel and lactic acid immediately made our complexions appear more radiant and firm, and, since lactic acid is a much milder exfoliator (it’s even safe enough to treat the symptoms of rosacea), it didn’t cause our sensitive skin to react. Instead, these antioxidants helped maintain moisture and worked alongside the other mild alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) like kojic acid to turn over dead cells while replenishing our skin with hydration, leaving it softer and smoother.

In fact, despite the gentle formula, we saw quicker results with this corrector than most of the other treatments we ever tried — as in two weeks fast, a short time compared to most formulas. Of course, we partially credit these results to our diligent use, never skipping a single morning or nighttime application through the full three months of testing. 

Still, we’re glad we stuck around because after six weeks our blemishes and acne scars had disappeared and we started to see the dark spots around our cheeks fade. Surely, the game-changing results are partially thanks to the formula’s use of phenylethyl resorcinol, a relatively new whitening agent derived from pine bark. It’s over 20 times more potent than kojic acid at inhibiting tyrosinase, the enzyme responsible for pigmenting skin and causing dark spots, and has higher antioxidant activity than vitamins E and C. 

Yet, what was more surprising was how well the formula cleared up our acne in spite of the fact that we stopped using our prescribed tretinoin. As we braced ourselves for an expected breakout, like around the time of our menstrual cycle, we were overjoyed when our skin remained as clear as ever.

As it turns out, research shows that azelaic acid works just as well in controlling acne, if not better, than tretinoin, and it’s less abusive, meaning not only were we experiencing fewer breakouts, but our skin was less aggravated, too. Also, silybum marianum fruit,  a plant-based extract that mitigates oil production, also helped inhibit new pimples from emerging, contributing to our renewed, refreshed complexions.

Truthfully, the only time we experienced discomfort was when we accidentally layered the serum across broken skin, so we learned to keep it away from any fresh cuts and only use it as a spot treatment on problem areas. 

Key Ingredients: Azelaic acid, kojic acid | Product Type: Serum | Benefits: Evens skin tone, clears acne, firms | Size: 1 oz

Best Budget

Versed Dark Spot Gel

Versed Skin

What We Love: Between in-office visits and high-tech laser treatment, getting rid of dark spots can be costly but this gel showed favorable results for less than $20.

What We Don’t Love: The formula takes a long time to absorb into the skin.

We won’t lie: It takes an aggressive approach to get rid of dark spots,often requiring expensive treatments and sometimes even multiple rounds of these treatments, so you can imagine our delight when this drugstore formula delivered exceptional results. Seriously, we noticed a reduction in our redness and hyperpigmentation soon after application, and an instant brightening effect that made our skin look like it was bathed in the soft lighting of an intimate dinner party. 

As luck, or science, would have it, the formula uses licorice root extract, which contains the chemical compound glabridin. This herbal remedy has quite the reputation for eliminating discoloration as it guards the skin against UVB-induced hyperpigmentation by dispersing the over-production of melanin and inhibiting unstable atoms known as free radicals from wreaking havoc (read: breaking down collagen) on otherwise healthy cells.

However, all good things take time, and our best skin yet was just around the corner. Around three weeks into treatment, the niacinamide calmed our rosacea-induced inflammation and the intensity of our breakouts subsided. Essentially, the vitamin B3 derivative acts as a blocker between keratinocytes, a cell found in the upper layer of the skin, and another one called melanocytes, regulating the signals in our skin calling for an increased transfer of melanosomes — AKA skin pigments.

It worked in tandem with tranexamic acid, an ingredient that’s shown promise in recent years to treat post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation and led us to see a significant reduction in dark spots by the end of our 12-week testing period. Not bad for treatment you can add to your cart while also shopping for toothpaste and paper towels, right? 

From its price to the proven, science-backed formula, this gel corrector showed up in some big ways, but if we’re getting nitpicky, we didn’t totally love how we had to wait a bit for its tacky residue to completely sink into our skin. Thankfully, the stickiness didn’t linger after the gel absorbed or caused our makeup to pill. 

Key Ingredients: Tranexamic acid, kojic acid, niacinamide, licorice root extract | Product Type: Gel | Benefits: Brightening | Size: 0.5 oz

Our Testing Process

After researching the best dark spot correctors available on the market, we narrowed down the list to the 13 most popular products currently available and brought InStyle editors to test the product in our NYC-based Lab. Then, each editor went home with a different dark spot corrector to test as part of our daily skincare routine for the next 12 weeks. We followed the manufacturer’s usage instructions and diligently applied the formula every day, sometimes twice, if the instructions called for it. 

Since dark spot correctors are a slow burn, we provided insights at three different check-in points: On day one, at six weeks, and at three months. Then we evaluated our results, ranking each one based on the texture and feel, the level of hydration it provided (or didn’t), any irritation it triggered and — not to sound vain — but how good our skin looked overall, if we had the radiance we wanted and the discoloration we didn’t. After analyzing the data, we ranked the best dark spot correctors that outperformed the others and the top scoring products make up this list.

The InStyle Team Tests Dark Spot Correctors

What to Keep in Mind

Formula

Manhattan-based board-certified dermatologist, Dr. Macrene Alexiades suggests looking for products that, “are working at the root of the matter” to repair the skin and avoid damage that causes brown spots and vulnerability, such as amino acids like glucosamine, aspartic acid, arginine, and citric acid. “Those are gentle and they're safe,” she says, adding that next you can seek out nourishing antioxidants, like a high dose of ferulic acid and vitamins C and E, that will repair the discolored damage without irritating the skin. 

Other mainstays, says New York City-based board-certified plastic and reconstructive surgeon, Dr. Umbareen Mahmood, are kojic acid, azelaic acid, tranexamic acid, niacinamide, and retinol. 

Skin Type

“People with acne-prone skin should avoid harsh ingredients and look to ones such as vitamin C, salicylic acid, azelaic acid, and tranexamic acid,” according to Dr. Mahmood. Similarly, she says those with sensitive skin should stick to formulas with natural ingredients, particularly kojic acid.

However, Dr. Alexiades, says that everyone, but especially darker skin tones and those with sensitive skin, should proceed with caution for products that contain hydroquinone, retinol, and glycolic acid, as they’re not easily tolerated. “If you're going to use glycolic acid or retinol, be aware, it's very irritating,” says Dr. Alexiades Even more surprising, “40 percent of adult women don't tolerate retinoids or glycolic acids,” adds Dr. Alexiades. “If you overuse them, your skin gets thin and it will make the skin weaker.” 

Skin Tone

In terms of skin tone, those with deeper colored skin should avoid harsh ingredients, especially hydroquinone, “as they can cause a white ring around the spot known as the ‘halo effect’,” explains Dr. Mahmood, who adds that these skin tones should “also avoid products with strong acids, or ingredients that can cause inflammation.” 

However, the best way to know for sure if a dark spot corrector is right for your skin type and tone is to consult your dermatologist, says master esthetician Sarah Akram. “There are different levels of melasma which means that the treatment or method of care will be different,” she adds, explaining that depending on your current skin routine, and what products you are using on your skin, some products make your skin more sensitive than others.

Your Questions, Answered 

What causes dark spots?

“There are a number of causes of dark spots, including sun damage, wounds, hormones, acne scarring, melasma, medication side effects, or certain medical conditions,” explains Dr. Mahmood.” Some studies have even shown that “prolonged exposure to blue light can lead to photoaging and the formation of dark spots.” 

Lifestyle choices aside, Dr. Alexiades says that some of us simply have a genetic predisposition to developing dark spots. “A lot of brown spots are associated with systemic disease and that is inherited,” she explains.  

How do you prevent dark spots from appearing?

Aside from trying to spend less time in direct sunlight, wearing protective coverings like a wide-brimmed sun hat and of course loading up on SPF are a couple of ways to avoid dark spots. 

You can also add a blue light screen protector to your devices. “We probably are accumulating some light damage just from looking at screens,” warns Dr. Alexiades. “Even if we have any light source in front of us, if we're in front of it long enough, sooner or later, those wavelengths are gonna get into the skin, to the DNA, and can cause trouble.” Just another reason why you should take a quick walk around the block every few hours while working from home.

Do dark spot correctors work?

The good news is that dark spot correctors work if you’re willing to put in the time. We can’t say this enough: Similar to laser hair removal or red light therapy, these treatments require patience and consistency, and it may be anywhere from four to eight before noticing significant changes in your skin, according to our experts. 

Although some formulas contain ingredients like vitamin C or hyaluronic acid, which could instantly provide a brightening or plumping effect, to get to the root of the issue and access long-term benefits, it's best to incorporate them into your daily beauty ritual. 

“Spot correctors are best applied as part of your morning routine, first by washing your face, then applying a spot corrector, followed by moisturizer and sunscreen,” recommends Dr. Mahmood.

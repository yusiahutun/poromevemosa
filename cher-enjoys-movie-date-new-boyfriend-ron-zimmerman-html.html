She may be approaching 65, but Cher is clearly not ready to slow down.

The iconic singer is soon to appear in a new movie, is working on a new album - and is also enjoying a new romance.

Cher stepped out yesterday hand-in-hand with boyfriend Ron Zimmerman, an American comedy writer in his 50s.

Scroll down to see the trailer for Cher's new movie

[caption]

It is the first time the pair have been seen out together, and it is understood they have been dating for about six months.

The romance follows Cher's trend of dating younger men - and comes after her split from her biker toy boy Tim Medvetz, 38.

The couple went to see new comedy Dinner for Schmucks, starring Paul Rudd and Steve Carell, in Calabasas California.

But the singer, dressed down in jeans, a tweed jacket and black hat, was clearly unimpressed - walking out before the film had finished.

[caption]

Her boyfriend is best known for writing Marvel Comics' Ultimate Adventure series, but has also had small roles in the drama 7th Heaven.

Zimmerman, whose curly mop of red curls rivalled his partner's long hair, is understood to be a close friend of American radio star Howard Stern, who broke the news of the couple's burgeoning romance.

Cher, who won an Oscar for Moonstruck in 1987, will soon return to the big screen alongside Christina Aguilera in the film Burlesque, due for release in November.

The diva plays former dancer, Tess, who runs a a neo-burlesque club and offers Aguilera's character Ali, a young orphaned girl, a job.

Cher took time out from her residency at Caesar's Palace in Las Vegas to film the movie, which also features two new songs.

[caption]

[caption]

She is working on her 26th studio album - her first release in 10 years.

It's understood she is working with hit-maker Dianne Warren, responsible for Aerosmith’s number one song I Don’t Wanna Miss a Thing and Celine Dion’s Because You Loved Me.

She said fans shouldn’t expect the same dance music heard on her 1998 album, Believe.

'It really won’t be Believe-ish,' she said recently.

'It will be more…I don’t know how to say it. I can’t put a genre to it. It’s a little bit more real instruments, like guitars and stuff like that.'

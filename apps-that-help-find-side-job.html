8 Apps That Will Help You Find a Side Job

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, January 31, 2024

Quick Links

  • Find Temporary Jobs With Wonolo
  • Veryable for Logistics, Manufacturing, and Warehousing
  • Tasker by TaskRabbit for Freelance Gigs
  • Hyer Job Search for Diverse Job Offerings
  • Instawork for Seasonal and Hospitality Work
  • Find a Flexible Side Job With Indeed Flex
  • Use Thumbtack for Professionals if You Have Home Improvement Skills
  • Upwork for Landing Freelance Clients

Many apps offer an easy way to find instant side jobs. All you need is your laptop or smartphone to look for temporary jobs that you can do in your free time. Download these helpful apps to help you find a side hustle.

1. Find Temporary Jobs With Wonolo

Close

Wonolo offers a convenient way for businesses and workers to connect through temporary jobs. Job searching is available across several cities in the US, and Wonolo says that they're working on expanding their reach. You can find legitimate temporary jobs in your area through the Wonolo app for iOS and Android.

Wonolo has strict rules for its workers. The app pays within three to five days, but only if the client verifies that you have completed the job. If you don't show up for a job, your account may be restricted, and Wonolo may remove your ability to accept future jobs.

Withdrawing from the job less than 12 hours before the start also gets you breach points. While the policy seems stringent, it gives workers credibility, which is an advantage for you if you decide to sign up.

If you want to unlock more jobs, follow Wonolo's pro-tips. Work on becoming a Preferred Wonoloer for more jobs to show up on your feed. Make sure that you also complete the following:

  • Turn on notifications.
  • Take the quick quiz about Wonolo's rules for workers.
  • Set up your payment method.
  • Attend a one-hour online class on app basics.
  • Complete your first job.

Download: Wonolo for Android | iOS (Free)

2. Veryable for Logistics, Manufacturing, and Warehousing

Close

Veryable helps you look for on-demand manufacturing, logistics, and warehousing jobs. If you become an operator or worker on the app, you get to work on flexible times and look for jobs in your area. The app works on a bidding system so that you can bid on tasks according to your schedule.

A shady online profile is one of the red flags employers look for when hiring candidates, so Veryable takes pains to conduct a background check on its operators. Give your full legal name, address, and other information for the required background check. Once you pass the background check, you'll be able to apply for a Vault account, where you'll receive your payments online. Veryable also asks for your W9 tax form.

Reviews say that Veryable pays quickly within two days and has responsive customer service. However, the number of opportunities depends on your area. If you get a good-paying gig, build a relationship with the company and stick with it.

Download: Veryable for Android | iOS (Free)

3. Tasker by TaskRabbit for Freelance Gigs

Close

Tasker by TaskRabbit might be the app for you if you're looking for the best places to find freelance work. TaskRabbit offers a variety of instant side jobs. Jobs range from desk jobs such as writing or accounting to more manual labor such as running errands, window cleaning, or indoor painting.

TaskRabbit made headlines with a Business Insider feature on four top-rated users who reportedly made six figures on the app. As a Tasker, you're not only working on a side job; you're growing your business. You can set your rates and availability. You can also choose to accept tasks on the same day.

Requirements may vary depending on your location. To get started as a Tasker, complete your online profile and pay the one-time $25 registration fee, which is applicable in some cities. If you're in California, TaskRabbit requires a business license to comply with local regulations.

Download: Tasker by TaskRabbit for Android | iOS (Free)

4. Hyer Job Search for Diverse Job Offerings

Close

Hyer offers users various side hustles. You can sign up as a bartender, cashier, dishwasher, in-store helper, pet sitter, landscaper, or more. To start making money, you need to attend an orientation, go through a background check, and set up a Stripe account where you'll get paid.

Hyer makes it easy to look for jobs and receive quick payments. You can search for jobs in 27 states across the US. Once you complete a task, you can request an Instant Payout or wait for the default weekly payout. The latter is processed every Monday and should get to your bank account no later than Thursday. However, bank holidays may affect this schedule, so read Hyer's guidelines if you need cash immediately.

Download: Hyer Job Search for Android | iOS (Free)

5. Instawork for Seasonal and Hospitality Work

Close

Instawork is a gig economy site that lets you land a decent side job if you have hospitality, culinary, and light industrial skills. Businesses post one-time or seasonal gigs, while workers pick shifts that fit their schedules. Instawork is the intermediary that takes care of all the paperwork and financial matters.

Here are some tips to best access the jobs at Instawork:

  • Complete your profile and work history.
  • Provide as much detail as possible in the employment section.
  • Ask for a recommendation from a former supervisor or co-worker to boost your profile.
  • Take advantage of the free training materials in the app. You can show these off in your profile and qualify for more opportunities.

Instawork sends payments to your bank account every Wednesday, and payment terms state that you only have to wait one to three days for the amount to show up. If you were hired as a W-2 worker, the amount you receive would be subject to taxes. If you need your payments ASAP, you can choose the Instapay feature to receive your compensation within a few hours of completing a task.

Download: Instawork for Android | iOS (Free)

6. Find a Flexible Side Job With Indeed Flex

Close

You may be familiar with Indeed, one of the best websites for hiring people online. In 2019, Indeed announced that it had acquired Syft, a UK-based staffing platform for part-time and flexible work. In 2020, the popular job site rebranded Syft and introduced Indeed Flex to the US. Indeed Flex is the app for you if you're looking for hospitality, retail, industrial, facilities, clerical, and retail roles.

To access open roles, download the app and sign up. From there, you can complete your profile and choose which jobs you'd like to explore. You'd have to get verified to look for more jobs. Find a role on the My Jobs page and apply, so you can book an onboarding interview to get verified. After this step, you'll be able to access more shifts.

Indeed Flex's terms and conditions state that you should receive weekly payments at the hourly rate you agreed to with your client. Indeed Flex then deposits your earnings into your chosen bank account. To ensure you get paid the right amount, submit accurate timesheets by clocking in and out of the mobile app.

Download: Indeed Flex for Android | iOS (Free)

7. Use Thumbtack for Professionals if You Have Home Improvement Skills

Close

Thumbtack for Professionals lets you book jobs and post listings instantly. This app is best for contractors who want to offer professional services like writing, home repair, massage therapy, cleaning, outdoor landscaping, tech repair, plumbing, furniture moving, and other odd jobs. Think of the usual tasks that eat up people's rest days. If you have the skills, sign up as a Thumbtack Professional.

Complete your profile so that clients can find you. Thumbtack helps you create a business profile that you can customize with your business name, description, and hours. While signing up is free, Thumbtack will prompt you to set up a weekly budget for direct leads. Once someone contacts you or books an appointment, you'll be charged for the initial communication.

Download: Thumbtack for Professionals for Android | iOS (Free)

8. Upwork for Landing Freelance Clients

Close

Upwork is worth checking out if you have some skills in a digital field, such as marketing or SEO. You'll find some higher-paying gems as you become more experienced, and you may also have the opportunity to pursue a full-time freelance career if that's something you think you would enjoy.

When using Upwork, you can create a profile from scratch or upload your resume. Moreover, you have the option to import a PDF of your LinkedIn profile. To help you choose the best clients, you can also see how much you should expect to earn per hour—along with whether the job poster has spent money on projects in the past.

Upwork takes 10% of what you earn, and you also need to pay for "Connects" that let you apply for jobs. Nonetheless, you can still find success if you optimize your profile, build your skills, and provide value. If you want to land more gigs on Upwork, check out these tips for boosting your Upwork clientele.

Download: Upwork for Android | iOS (Free, in-app purchases available)

Many temporary job apps are available if you need extra income. Choose the best app for your skills and resources, considering when and how you'll get paid. Then, use the app to earn quick cash or turn your side hustle into a business. Remember to review the profiles of each employer as well. While the apps do a background check, it doesn't hurt to be cautious about online job scams.

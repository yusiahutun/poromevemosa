1. Jeffrey once worked as a weatherman

Jeffrey Hewson was born in Dayton, Ohio, on August 5, 1951, and grew up raised nearby Franklin. Prior to joining QVC, he worked as a radio disc jockey and TV weatherman in Dayton.

Then, in 1983, he moved to Pennsylvania to work for a local restaurant, where he discovered a local cable network QVC. He saw an opening for an on-air presenter and applied, and after a few auditions, he landed the job. It was the start of a remarkable career that would make him a household name across the United States.

2. He became a QVC host in 1986

Jeffrey became a QVC host in 1986, becoming one of its most iconic TV presenters. His charming personality and quick wit made him bond closely with his audiences. In addition, he was renowned for his demonstrations of products, some of which were slightly absurd.

What happened to Jeffrey Hewson on QVC?

He disappeared from the screen, making viewers wonder what had happened to their favourite host. Rumours started circulating that he was no longer with the network, but QVC provided no clarifications.

The silence from QVC added to the mystery surrounding his departure. In a 2017 interview with the New York Times, former QVC colleague Lisa Robertson shed some light on the situation. Lisa revealed that Jeffrey had suffered a stroke, which left him partially paralysed and unable to continue working at QVC.

Jeffrey's suffered a stroke right around the time anchor Lisa Roberston joined QVC, and she saw him struggle to recover. She recalled how he had to re-learn how to speak and walk. So where is Jeffrey Hewsen? After some time, he chose to retire and left the state of Pennsylvania, where QVC is headquartered.

Jeffrey Hewson is an iconic salesman, and his story perfectly exemplifies his fall from fate to grace. He has kept a low profile, and little is known about his current whereabouts.

Henry B. Eyring: 'You make choices every day and almost every hour that keep you walking in the ligh

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, January 1, 0001

You make choices every day and almost every hour that keep you walking in the light or moving away toward darkness.

In his quote, Henry B. Eyring beautifully captures the essence of our lives: the constant battle between light and darkness. He suggests that every day, and almost every hour, we are confronted with choices that guide us either toward the light or plunge us into darkness. This quote serves as a reminder of the power we hold within ourselves to shape our paths. It implies that the outcome of our journey is not predetermined, but rather influenced by the choices we make.At first glance, this quote presents a straightforward message urging us to be mindful of our decisions and their potential consequences. It encourages us to evaluate the choices we make and the direction they lead us in. If we consistently opt for actions that align with moral values, kindness, and integrity, we stay on the path of light. However, should we succumb to temptation, deceit, or harmful behavior, we find ourselves drifting towards the darkness. This interpretation emphasizes the importance of personal accountability and the impact our daily choices have on our overall character.But let's dive deeper into the philosophical implications of Eyring's quote. The idea that our choices determine our path aligns with the philosophical concept of existentialism. According to existentialism, individuals create their own meaning and purpose in life through their choices and actions. Jean-Paul Sartre, a prominent existentialist philosopher, famously asserted that "existence precedes essence," suggesting that we exist as blank slates, devoid of predetermined purpose, until we make choices that define who we are.Drawing from this concept, Eyring's quote highlights the crucial role our choices play in shaping not only our individual paths but also our very essence. It emphasizes that darkness is not an external force that engulfs us passively; rather, it is the outcome of our own decisions. By continuously making choices that align with the light, we actively create a life that reflects our values and aspirations.However, introducing existentialism into the discussion also provides an interesting contrast to Eyring's quote. While both perspectives acknowledge the significance of choices, existentialism promotes the idea of radical freedom, suggesting that we have the ultimate power to shape our lives. On the other hand, Eyring's quote emphasizes a moral dimension, implying a higher power guiding our journey. It suggests that the light we choose to walk in encompasses divine or universal truths that inherently lead to happiness and fulfillment.In this sense, Eyring's quote transcends a mere philosophical reflection on choices and delves into the realm of spirituality. It presents a belief that our choices not only shape our lives but also influence our spiritual well-being. By aligning our actions with goodness, compassion, and love, we strengthen our connection to a higher power or universal truth. It is through these choices that we find a profound sense of purpose and meaning in our existence.In conclusion, Henry B. Eyring's quote reminds us of the power we hold in our hands with each choice we make. It urges us to be mindful of the direction our choices steer us, highlighting that they can either keep us walking in the light or draw us toward darkness. While straightforward in its message, the quote also invites us to explore the philosophical concept of existentialism, providing an intriguing contrast between personal freedom and moral alignment. Ultimately, this quote encourages us to become conscious architects of our lives, shaping our paths and spiritual well-being through the choices we make each day.

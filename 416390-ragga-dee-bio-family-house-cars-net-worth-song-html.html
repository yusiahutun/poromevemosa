Ragga Dee is not a new name in Uganda as he wears different hats. He is a talented singer, successful businessman, political aspirant, loving husband and caring father. He rose to prominence in the early 1990s and has remained a great personality to date.

Ragga Dee biography

Ragga Dee was born in 1973, but he has not offered any information about the specific month and date of birth. His birth name is Daniel Kazibwe. He is the son of Mr. and Mrs. George William Kyeyune. He has not offered any information about his siblings.

Ragga is a well-educated man, as seen in the several business courses he has undertaken. First, he studied business at Makerere University Business School. Later, he joined Tennessee Valley University in Atlanta, Georgia. Eventually, he enrolled at Elephant and Castle Business School in London.

Career

Ragga started his career in music in 1988. He majored in hip hop, ragga, reggae, and Lingala. However, he rose to prominence in the early 1990s after releasing several hit songs.

Business

While most singers solely cling to music as their source of income and career, the singer has chosen a different path. He is a successful businessman with several money-making ventures in his name. For instance, he is a top luxury car dealer in Uganda.

Political ambitions

The self-proclaimed grandfather of all Ugandan artists is a politically ambitious personality. He tried his luck in politics in 2015, as he sought to be elected for the mayoral post of Kampala. He considered himself fit for the position, having been born and raised in Kampala.

I have been in Kampala long enough to know what Kampala people want and are missing. I was born in 1973 and am of age to lead people.

He also clarified his educational background during the campaigning period. He stated that he was a graduate of Tennessee Valley University in the United States.

Ragga Dee's wife and children

The singer is married to the love of his life, Mariam Kazibwe. After living together for many years, the singer decided to formalize their union at the Namirembe Cathedral. Later, the couple hosted their guests at the Serena Hotel after its rebranding from Nile Hotel.

The wedding ceremony was one of the most expensive ones held in the country. The singer bought three Escalades and two limousines for the bridal team instead of hiring. The wife maintains a low profile, and she is rarely seen on tabloids or in public.

Together, the couple has been blessed with four lovely children. They have two daughters and two sons. Unlike his other children, his firstborn daughter Daphine Elise Kazibwe has made it to the public's domain on numerous occasions.

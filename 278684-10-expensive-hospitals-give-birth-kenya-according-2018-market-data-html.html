10 most expensive hospitals to give birth in Kenya according to new 2018 market data

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, March 9, 2024

- The least you will part with when you deliver in any of these hospitals is KSh 45,000

- The highest you will pay for delivery through caesarean section is KSh 280,000

- The inflated cost of birth in private health facilities was attributed to new taxes on medical equipment

- Doctors' constant push for higher salaries has also been blamed for the skyrocketing cost of delivery

The cost of giving birth in Kenya's leading private hospitals has significantly increased and the recently introduced taxes on medical equipment are partly to blame.

The least one can part with at any of the private health facilities is KSh 45,000 for normal delivery and the highest is KSh 280,000 for delivery through caesarean section.

A fresh 2018 market report by AON Kenya Insurance Brokers seen by TUKO.co.ke on Wednesday, July 4, indicated the cost of delivery had skyrocketed beyond expectations.

According to the report, Aga Khan Hospital Nairobi was the most expensive private health facility with normal birth costing KSh 150, 000 and caesarean section delivery at KSh 280,000.

The Mater Hospital and M.P. Shah Hospital came second and third respectively in terms of how much it would cost a mother to give birth in these hospitals.

Below is the complete list of the top 10 most expensive private hospitals to give birth according to the AON Kenya Insurance Brokers 2018 market report:

1. Aga Khan Hospital Nairobi

Normal delivery costs one KSh 150, 000, whereas cesarean section attracts KSh 280,000.

2. Mater Hospital

Normal delivery fee at this hospital is KSh 80,000 while one will part with KSh 220,000 for cesarean section.

3. M.P. Shah Hospital

Normal delivery is KSh 120,000 while C-section goes for KSh 220,000.

4. Karen Hospital

Normal delivery KSh 130,000, cesarean section is KSh 230,000.

5. Nairobi Hospital

Normal delivery goes for KSh 180,000, cesarean section is at KSh 220,000.

6. Nairobi Women's Hospital

Normal delivery is KSh 64,000. Cesarean section is KSh 145,000.

7. Coptic Hospital

Normal delivery is KSh 45,000 while cesarean section is KSh 120,000.

8. Avenue hospital

Normal delivery will see one forking out KSh 60,000 while Cesarean section goes for KSh 220,000.

9. Nairobi West hospital

Normal delivery is at KSh 60,000 while cesarean section costs KSh 220,000

10. Nairobi South Medical Centre Ltd.

Normal delivery is KSh 80,000 while CS is at KSh 180,000.

Besides the newly introduced taxes, the inflated cost of maternity services at the named private health facilities was also linked to constant push for higher pay by doctors.

The new high costs are likely to deny most Kenyans access to private hospitals.

The other options would be to either go to the public health facilities or use the services of traditional birth attendants.

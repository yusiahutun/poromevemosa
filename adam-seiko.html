Adam Seiko Bio, Age, Height, Parents, Girlfriend, Wiki

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, July 14, 2024

Adam Seiko is an American basketball player who currently plays for San Diego State Aztecs in NCAA as a point guard. In the game, he averaged 1.5 rebounds and 6.7 points. Adam is an emerging player who could do wonders in the professional scene if he gets a chance. He has only played at college levels and will soon start playing basketball professionally.

Early Life

Adam Seiko was born on 27 February 1998 in Boston. He graduated from Sierra Canyon High, California. He is of mixed origin in America and Uganda. His mother Eva Saira Ariko is his biggest supporter. Adam has a brother named Arthur Kaluma who is also a basketball player. They have also played in the same team. Their mother has always supported them in their career and she motivates them to do their best. She never misses any match and is always present to cheer her sons.

Adam Seiko Career

Adam started playing basketball in high school. He was always passionate about the game. His hard work earned him the No.5 rank at the state and the No.15 rank at the national level in the year 2016-17. He helped his high school team reach to semi-finals in The California Interscholastic Federation tournament in 2017. His outstanding performance at the state level earned him Los Angeles Daily News all-area first-team honors as a senior. He spent four years playing on his Sierra Canyon High School team.

Adam got redshirted in his freshman year in the 2016-17 season. He played 32 games in the next season and made his collegiate debut. In his sophomore year, he appeared in 29 games all off the bench. In 2020-21 he appeared in 27 games as a junior and appeared in 25 games as a senior. He recorded his career-high points vs Creighton in the NCAA tournament. He is currently playing for the University of San Diego State Aztec.

Adam Seiko is most recently seen in Mountain West Tournament. Being part of the Aztecs team is the best part of his collegiate career. Adam made his Silverbacks debut in a match against Senegal. He got a degree in interdisciplinary studies. He is currently enrolled in a master’s program at the school of psychology.

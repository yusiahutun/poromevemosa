14 hidden roof designs that are beautiful and cheaper than flat roofs

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, June 1, 2024

Architecture has evolved to incorporate various creative designs from multiple cultures. One such popular design borrowed from Japanese architecture is the hidden roof. Though different from the original Japanese architecture, modern hidden roof designs are minimalist and simple.

Hidden roof designs have grown in popularity in recent times. Building owners love them for their affordability, aesthetics and functionality. In addition, you can incorporate various styles that suit your preferences best.

14 hidden roof designs

Hidden roof designs are trendy as they offer a seamless appearance through their visual impact. The roof design also provides various functionalities, such as better energy efficiency, insulation, and resistance to the elements.

According to Makazi and similar websites, here are 14 hidden roof designs with pictures.

1. Flat hidden roof

The flat hidden roof is one of the hidden roof designs in Kenya. It features an entirely flat roof that is not visible from the ground. Among its benefits are ease of construction and affordability.

2. Green or living roof

As the name suggests, the green hidden roof features soil and vegetation. This design's concept is to create wildlife habitats while providing insulation and privacy to your residence. In addition, the vegetation reduces rapid water run-off, thus preserving the structure.

3. Hidden roof with parapets

The hidden roof with parapets is the most common and widely used design. It features parapets and wall extensions at the edge that extend to provide a safety barrier. The extension also gives the building a sophisticated and more finished appearance.

4. Roof with skylights

A roof with skylights is among this decade's most modern hidden roof designs. The concept involves installing skylights into the hidden roof to allow natural light into the building. 5. Roof with solar panels

Incorporating solar panels into your roofing is one of Kenya's most popular hidden roof designs. It involves integrating the solar panels into the roof design, making them less noticeable. This eco-friendly design is ideal for those looking to save costs and energy.

6. Hidden roof with overhangs

A roof overhang, also called the eaves, is a bit of roofing that protrudes past the exterior walls. Overhangs can hide the top from certain angles while providing shade and protecting the building's exterior from rain.

7. Terrace or patio extension

The terrace or patio is an externally raised and open flat area that extends the hidden roof. You can utilise this space as an additional living or leisure space. Here, people can eat, drink and enjoy while enjoying the outside views.

8. Roof with clerestory windows

If you are looking for hidden roof designs for your house, incorporating clerestory windows is a creative idea. Clerestory are vertical rows of windows at the top of a wall that, when open, use their height to reek out warm air from the building's interior. These windows also let in light while keeping the roof hidden from view.

9. Hidden roof with chimney

You can include a chimney in your hidden roof house plans. However, there are factors you should consider before doing so. First, use lap sealant on all edges and intersections to avoid leakage. You could also seek the services of a qualified roofing contractor for the best results.

10. Multi-level hidden roof

The multi-level hidden roof house design is stylish and aesthetically pleasing. The contemporary home design features a modular-like maisonette with hidden roofs for every level.

11. Middle gutter hidden roof

The middle gutter hidden roof design features a unique drainage system that involves two parts of the roof converging in the middle. At the centre, the gutter system directs any rainwater to the roof's edge to drain.

12. Slate tiles

Slate tiles are premium roofing products with exceptional durability and low maintenance. They are also non-absorbent and require no sealant. Even when used on a hidden roof, slate tiles still provide the best durability and functionality.

13. Mono pitched

Mono-pitched roofing is ideal if you want the best-hidden roof construction idea. It is very effective as it drains rainwater and snow quickly. The roof's slight incline creates more indoor space and is stylish.

14. Contemporary hidden roof

The contemporary hidden roof offers the latest designs in architectural trends. It is perfect for people looking to stand out and be trendsetters. The design features a slanting flat roof that offers clean and unobstructed aesthetics.

Wrapping up

Hidden roof designs have gained popularity over time due to their aesthetics and functionality. The design is prevalent in contemporary and minimalist architecture. One of the most significant advantages of the hidden roof is its cost and sleek appearance.

Mother's heartbreak as distressing video of her son, 39, who took his own life is shared online

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, August 10, 2024

A heartbroken mother whose son took his own life says the man who posted a video of his body online 'amplified' her grief and committed the 'ultimate insult'.

Daniel Knott, 39, died on March 27. His body was discovered by a passer by who called emergency services for help.

But before first responders arrived, Jacob Morse managed to take video of the scene. He later shared the footage online, causing the Knott family serious distress.

Mr Knott's mother, Jenny Tancock, told BBC she felt 'pure anger' and 'disbelief' after learning of the video. She could not comprehend how 'somebody could be so vile'.

Morse, 22, pleaded guilty to circulating a video on social media that was grossly offensive or of an indecent, obscene or menacing character at Llanelli Magistrates' Court. On May 18 he was sentenced to six weeks in prison.

Daniel Knott, 39, died on March 27. His heartbroken mother Jenny Tancock says the man who posted a video of Mr Knott's body online 'amplified' her grief and committed the 'ultimate insult'. Mr Knott and Ms Tancock are pictured together

Ms Tancock, from Carmarthenshire, slammed Morse for further harming her family during a time when 'we should just be grieving for Daniel'.

'[I felt] anger, pure anger, disbelief that somebody could be so vile as to arrive at a scene like that and actually video it,' she said, adding that sharing the video was the 'ultimate degrading of someone'.

The mother, who has not watched the video, said that Morse's actions left her feeling 'full of hate'. She is understood to be 'very torn' about his six-week custodial sentence.

She argued that six weeks is not a long enough sentence, but she knows her son would not have wanted Morse jailed.

'It hit me as a mother, it really did, he just looked like a big kidâ€¦ and I was really torn as to how I felt,' she shared. 'I kept thinking 'what would Dan do?' and I don't think Dan would have wanted him to go to prison.'

Jacob Morse pleaded guilty to circulating a video on social media that was grossly offensive or of an indecent, obscene or menacing character at Llanelli Magistrates' Court (pictured). On May 18 he was sentenced to six weeks in prison

She shared how Mr Knott had been 'an enigma, full of beans, full of life' since his childhood, but claims that changed after a car accident seven years ago caused him to lose everything.

The crash left Mr Knott with a fractured skull and a broken back that required surgery. He also lost his home, job and partner as he battled the long-term impacts of the incident.

Ms Tancock hopes sharing his story will 'raise awareness' and prevent anyone else from sharing footage 'traumatic' footage online.

'These are human beings, they're people, they've got families, to share things on the internet for shock value or for likes is awful and I want them to know that there's consequences,' she added.

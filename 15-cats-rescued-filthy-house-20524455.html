15 cats rescued from filthy house 'like something out of a horror movie'

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, September 12, 2024

Fifteen cats have been found in “horrific” conditions after their owner was taken into hospital.

The animals were left uncared-for for around a month before volunteers from Gainsborough Feral Rescue entered the property to investigate.

Melanie Barker, who runs the rescue, told Lincolnshire Live that a local vet had got in touch with her after the elderly lady was hospitalised.

"I never expected to find the cats in the state I did,” she said.

"It was horrible, it was like something out of a horror movie. We were only going to have a look because we didn't have much space, but when we got there we realised we couldn't leave them in there."

Melanie says that the cats were “traumatised” with some rooms of the house absolutely swimming in cat urine and faeces.

"I wish the photos could've conveyed the smell, it just made you sick and made your eyes water," she said.

"We could tell some of these cats had been sitting in their own urine and faeces because the fur on the back of their legs had been worn away. "

"Some of them have got teeth and jaw problems, some have kidney issues, some are underfed and underweight.

"A lot of them are pretty traumatised - they cower away from you and won't come out to feed unless they're sheltered and feel safe."

Melanie says that her organisation desperately needs help coping with the sudden influx of cats that need food, veterinary care and – ultimately – new homes.

"I'm guessing the person looking after them thought she was doing right, but they were appalling conditions," she said.

"Some of these cats have been taken off the street - we've actually reunited one cat with their owner after it disappeared four years ago.

"A lot of these cats need dentals and we are facing huge vet bills. We've had a lot of support - people have donated £700 already which is fantastic."

But the story may not be over yet – Melanie fears that there are more cats waiting to be discovered: "The person involved has been known to move cats about if she knows the authorities are going to check on them," she said.

10 types of prayer in Christianity and when to say them

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, September 19, 2024

Prayer is one of the most powerful tools for Christians. Through it, Christians communicate with their creator, ask for their needs and give thanks for what they have. Prayers are personal and intimate. There are different types of prayer for various occasions and seasons. Happy times, sad times, conflicted times, all time is devotion time for a true believer.

A prayer can be either long or short. God listens to different kinds of prayers no matter your situation. The various prayer categories help Christians choose the right type of prayers and say them.

Types of prayer in Christianity and when to say them

When communicating with God, remember to focus your energy on the prayer item. Here are ten different types of prayers in Christianity and when to say them:

1. Prayer of healing

This is one of the many kinds of Christian prayers. It is said when one asks for their physical strength back. You can also pray for spiritual healing, healing of emotional wounds, or psychological issues.

2. Adoration prayer

Prayer of adoration is said by Christians to worship the Lord and get to appreciate His love. It is also said to show the respect and admiration Christians have for God and His son Jesus Christ. The prayer of adoration can be said at any time.

3. Prayer of deliverance

Many Christians pray for a breakthrough at some point in their lives. The prayer for deliverance is best suited when one is going through a difficult time and wants God to come through for them. Christians are encouraged to say prayers of redemption. God is the one who can assist everyone in ways no one else ever could.

5. Prayer of intercession

When praying, it is important to remember others too. Do not just pray for yourself but remember friends, acquaintances, family, and those in need. As a good Christian, you are reminded to pray for one another and intercede on someone else's behalf. Just like Jesus prayed for others in His final hours before being arrested, remember to mention others when you kneel to talk to God.

6. Confession prayer

Confession prayer is for acknowledging one's sin. By talking of your transgressions to God, you get forgiveness and start afresh. By confessing your sins, asking for forgiveness, and sinning no more, Christians draw closer to God.

7. Prayer to saints

For the longest time, ancient churches in both Eastern Christianity and Western Christianity prayed for the intercession of saints. This tradition was prevalent for Christians that attend Eastern Orthodox, Oriental Orthodox, and Roman Catholic churches. Some Lutherans and Anglicans also dedicate prayers to saints. This practice is, however, rare in many reformed churches.

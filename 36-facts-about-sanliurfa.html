Sanliurfa is also known as “The City of Prophets.”

Legend has it that this ancient city was the birthplace of numerous prophets, including Abraham, making it a significant religious site for Muslims, Christians, and Jews.

It is home to the world-famous Balikligol (Abraham’s Pool).

This sacred pool is believed to be the spot where King Nimrod threw Abraham into the fire, only for it to miraculously turn into water and the logs into fish.

Sanliurfa has a history dating back to the Neolithic Age.

Archaeological excavations have discovered ancient settlements and artifacts that showcase the city’s rich cultural heritage.

The city boasts stunning historical architecture.

Sanliurfa is renowned for its delicious cuisine.

Indulge in mouthwatering kebabs, grilled meats, and local delicacies such as the famous Urfa pepper, known for its distinct flavor and spice.

The Turkish TV series “Dirilis: Ertugrul” was filmed in Sanliurfa.

This epic historical drama gained international popularity and showcased the beauty of the city to a global audience.

Sanliurfa is home to the world’s oldest temple complex.

Gobekli Tepe, a UNESCO World Heritage site, dates back over 11,000 years and provides fascinating insights into early human civilization.

The ancient city of Harran is located near Sanliurfa.

Harran is famous for its astonishing beehive-shaped houses and is believed to be one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world.

FAQs

1. What are some must-see attractions in Sanliurfa?

Göbekli Tepe, the Pool of Abraham, and the Urfa Castle are some of the must-see attractions in Sanliurfa. These sites offer a glimpse into the city’s history and are of significant cultural and historical importance.

2. What is the best time to visit Sanliurfa?

The best time to visit Sanliurfa is during the spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November) seasons. The weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds compared to the summer months.

Yes, Sanliurfa is known for its religious significance. The Pool of Abraham is believed to be the birthplace of the prophet Abraham, making it an important site for both Muslims and Christians.

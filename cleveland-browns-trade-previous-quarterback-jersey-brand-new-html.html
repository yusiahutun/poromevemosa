Cleveland Browns fans can trade in their old quarterbacks' jersey from 1999 to 2021 for a brand-new

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, August 26, 2024

Cleveland Browns fans can trade in a jersey of one of the team's former starting quarterbacks, who was under center during the 21st century, in a swap only available before tonight's game against the Jets. 

There are 31 former Browns who are options to have their jersey traded in, and be replaced with a jersey of one of four current Cleveland players â€” Joe Flacco, Deshaun Watson, Nick Chubb, and Myles Garrett. 

Both Chubb and Watson are out for the season, with Flacco earning the current starting quarterback role for the rest of the season, even though he's likely to be released by the team by March.  


Some of the options for trading in include Johnny Manziel, Robert Griffin III, Baker Mayfield, Brady Quinn, and Doug Pederson. 

Every quarterback to start for the team since Tim Couch's selection as the No. 1 overall pick in the 1999 NFL Draft until the end of the 2021 season is eligible. 

Joe Flacco is the team's quarterback for the rest of the season, replacing Deshaun Watson

Johnny Manziel failed to live up to the hype in Cleveland and now his jersey can be traded away

Manziel was drafted in the first round in 2014, but was out of the NFL by the end of 2015 

The promotion is being run by DUDE Wipes, a reusable toilet paper company marketed to men. 

DUDE Wipes appeared on ABC's entrepreneurial show 'Shark Tank' in 2015 where they secured a $300,000 investment from former Dallas Mavericks owner Mark Cuban for 25% of their company. 

The only sizes available are large, extra large, and 2XL. And there will only be about 100 jerseys available. 

With the limited quantity, Browns fans shouldn't need three hours to throw away the jerseys of so many failed starters. 

The Cleveland Browns play the New York Jets at home tonight, where a victory would clinch them a playoff spot for the first time since 2020. 

A postseason berth would only be the franchise's second since 2003, the span when a heavy majority of the quarterback's whose jerseys are being traded in played for the team. 

The team's longest-term quarterback during that stretch was Baker Mayfield, who played for the team from 2018-21.

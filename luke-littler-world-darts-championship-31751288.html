Luke Littler 'boy wonder of darts' is 'better than Michael van Gerwen at the same age'

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, July 3, 2024

Luke Littler arrived at Alexandra Palace as the boy wonder of darts – but on Wednesday, on a bill featuring the world's top two, he is the main man.

‌Although Michael Smith continues the defence of his Paddy Power PDC World Championship crown against Madars Razma, and Michael van Gerwen should blow away Richard Veenstra, 16-year-old Littler will be centre stage again.

‌And Luke the Nuke goes into his third round contest against Canada's Matt Campbell with a gallery of champions lining up to acclaim him as the real deal. Glen Durrant, Mark Webster, Gary Anderson, 'Bully Boy' Smith and Peter Wright have won seven world titles between them, and none of them doubt the Warrington whizzkid's natural talent.

‌Duzza, who won three Lakeside world championships and the PDC Premier League on his debut, was the most effusive, celebrating Littler as a “once-in-a-generation” talent, while Snakebite Wright is convinced he is the “future of darts.”

‌Here's what the jury of legends had to say about the manchild who has taken Ally Pally by storm:

Can Luke Littler win the current World Darts Championship? Tell us in the comments section below

Glen Durrant

“I am a big, big fan of Luke Littler. He’s got something special, he has that real X-Factor. I remember watching Michael van Gerwen when I first went on the circuit. He was 16 years old and I thought, 'This is is a once-in-a-generation player' - and I think Luke Littler at 16 is better than Van Gerwen was at the same age.

‌“He has excellent rhythm, he’s very mature, he looks older than 16 and he has handled it very, very well so far.

‌“There is a lot of scrutiny, but he just brushes it aside. The confidence that is coming out of him right now, it’s a good time for him to go to Ally Pally. He is on a roll, so why not go and enjoy it?”

‌Mark Webster

‌“He’s an ambitious 16-year-old, he doesn’t want to be hanging around on the development tours too long. He wants to be in the top 32 in a couple of years and I think he will have a good tournament.

‌“I thought he would get past Christian Kist and Andrew Gilding, who won major tournaments on the big stage, and it’s going to be different as there is going to be a bit more coming back at him now.

‌“But look, he has the head for it, he has the game, he has a plan. I don’t think he is going to be impatient and think he is going to be the world champion - he is going to go with the flow.

‌“It is a great bonus for him. He knows he has a Tour card for 2024, his career is mapping out, and in a way the World Championship is like a bonus event.”

‌Gary Anderson

‌“I don’t think I’ve met Luke but aye, cracking, brilliant. I never even started playing darts until I was about 24.

‌“The youngsters nowadays are coming from school straight into darts, which is great. They can have a few good years, but what’s going to happen when it starts going wrong?

‌“Have you got the jewels to say, ‘I’m going for it, I am going to make a living out of darts'? It’s a hard call especially if you’ve got a family. But this lad can play.”

‌Michael Smith

‌“I actually spoke about Luke with Nathan (Aspinall) this week. He is with the same management team. For me, he is good – like, really good.

‌“But I just don’t want to see the hype that Josh Rock got last year, and he has struggled a bit this year. I don’t want to put too much pressure on Luke, especially being 16 years old, but I don’t think he has ever lost on stage at the PDC.

‌“He has played on it four times, in two world youth finals and stuff, and he has always played really well. But being on the Tour with another 127 people, who will be throwing 95 averages or 100 averages, will be different.

“Just leave him alone. Just let him get on with it. Let him find his feet... but he is good.”

‌Peter Wright

‌“When I was 16, I was playing county level for London. Yes, Luke Littler is magic, a brilliant dart player.That’s the future of darts, him and Gian Van Veen. They are going to be really good.”
